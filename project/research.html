Virtual Reality Therapy

As technology continues to evolve and develop, companies become aware of the desire for consumers to buy technologies that will entertain and make daily life better. Virtual reality technology has become more affordable and accessible to the public in recent years due to demand and the development of better technology. Although virtual reality is typically used for entertainment, one of its many uses can assist therapy patients in their treatments. This technology provides a more cost-effective, realistic experience that does not have to physically place a patient in a new or unfamiliar surrounding, allowing them to be comfortable at all times.

Virtual Reality can be used to treat conditions such as:

  • Social Anxiety Disorder
  • Fear of Flying
  • Panic disorder
  • Social Phobia
  • Claustrophobia
  • Arachnophobia
  • Body dysmorphic disorder
  • PTSD.. and many more
​As a treatment for these disorders, exposure therapy is usually done by:
  • In vivo exposure: where the patient directly faces the feared situation. This method is effective, but it is costly, time-consuming, and difficult to control.
  • Imaginary exposure: the patient imagines the feared situation with help from pictures or videos. Imaginary exposure may lack realism and intensity, and can especially be difficult for people who find it hard to imaginate situations.

Patients who are skeptical of biomedical risks from treatments, gravitate towards holistic and/or “natural” health and wellness models, such as meditation, yoga, naturopathy, or biofeedback, something that VR therapy can provide. Leisure experiences through Virtual Reality result in enhanced mental wellbeing, effective coping with daily routines, stresses, and roles. Leisure experiences may include activities such as walking along side a lake, through the woods, or meditating in a field.

The audio component for VR experiences typically involve exposure to a standardized guided meditation invoking a relaxing scenario such as a natural experience accompanied by repetitive positive affirmations and mantras to enhance a participant‟s self-esteem or psychological outlook. White noises are also key components to making sure an experience will let the user feel completely immersed in the surroundings. Without sound, the experience becomes much less effective, therefore, it is one of the most important elements to focus on.
